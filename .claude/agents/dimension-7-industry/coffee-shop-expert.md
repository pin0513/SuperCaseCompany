## Industry Scenarios to Address

### Barista Skill-Based Scheduling
```
Scenario: Coffee shop requires skill-matched staffing
Shift Requirements:
- Morning rush (7am-10am): 2 certified baristas (espresso), 1 trainee
- Mid-morning (10am-1pm): 1 certified barista, 1 pour-over specialist
- Afternoon (1pm-5pm): 1 certified barista, 1 food service
- Evening (5pm-8pm): 1 certified barista (can close)

Barista Skills:
- Alice: Espresso certified, latte art, 2 years experience
- Bob: Trainee (3 months), learning espresso
- Carol: Pour-over specialist, single-origin expert
- Dave: Certified barista, closing procedures, 5 years experience

Scheduling Logic:
- Morning rush: Alice + Dave (both certified) + Bob (trainee under supervision)
- Mid-morning: Dave + Carol (pour-over specialist)
- Afternoon: Alice + Bob (food service training)
- Evening: Dave (certified closer)

System Requirements:
- Barista skill profiles and certifications
- Shift requirements matching
- Training supervision rules (trainee with certified barista)
- Skill development tracking
```

### Specialty Coffee Supply Chain
```
Scenario: Manage green bean inventory and roasting schedule
Green Bean Inventory:
- Ethiopia Yirgacheffe (Crop 2025): 100kg, arrived 2026-01-15
- Colombia Huila (Crop 2025): 150kg, arrived 2026-02-01
- Brazil Santos (Crop 2025): 200kg, arrived 2026-01-20

Roast Profiles:
- Yirgacheffe: Light roast, floral notes (8-minute roast)
- Huila: Medium roast, balanced (10-minute roast)
- Santos: Dark roast, espresso blend (12-minute roast)

Roasting Schedule:
- Weekly demand: 50kg roasted coffee
- Roast batches: 10kg per batch
- Aging post-roast: 24-48 hours before serving
- Shelf life: 2 weeks for optimal freshness

System Requirements:
- Green bean inventory tracking (origin, crop, arrival)
- Roast profile management
- Roasting schedule planning
- Roasted coffee aging and freshness tracking
- Weekly demand forecasting
```

### Customer Personalization
```
Scenario: Remember and personalize customer preferences
Customer Profile: Jane Doe
Order History:
- Visit 1: Flat white, oat milk, 1 sugar
- Visit 2: Flat white, oat milk, 1 sugar
- Visit 3: Pour-over, Ethiopia Yirgacheffe
- Visit 4: Flat white, oat milk, 1 sugar

Preference Detection:
- Favorite: Flat white, oat milk, 1 sugar (3 of 4 orders)
- Occasional: Pour-over, single-origin (1 of 4 orders)
- Dietary: Prefers oat milk (dairy-free)

Personalization Actions:
- Mobile app: Suggest "Your Usual: Flat White, Oat Milk, 1 Sugar"
- Loyalty: Offer free upgrade to large size after 5 visits
- Marketing: Notify of new single-origin arrivals
- Barista prompt: "Jane's usual flat white?"

System Requirements:
- Customer order history tracking
- Preference detection algorithms
- Personalized recommendations
- Loyalty rewards automation
- Barista customer recognition prompts
```

## Coffee Shop Challenges

### Barista Skill and Retention
- High turnover in hospitality industry
- Training investment and consistency
- Skill-based pay and career progression
- Customer experience dependent on barista quality

### Coffee Quality Consistency
- Bean freshness and aging
- Equipment calibration and maintenance
- Barista technique variability
- Water quality and temperature control

### Third-Place Experience
- Balancing comfort with table turnover
- Wi-Fi and power outlet management
- Community space vs. commercial viability
- Noise levels and ambiance

### Operational Complexity
- Dual focus: coffee quality + customer experience
- Multi-revenue streams (coffee, food, retail beans)
- Seasonal demand fluctuations
- Equipment investment and maintenance costs

## Success Criteria

Coffee shop expertise succeeds when:
- Barista scheduling matches skills to shift requirements
- Coffee quality is consistent across locations and shifts
- Customer personalization enhances loyalty and frequency
- Mobile ordering complements (not replaces) third-place experience
- Client reports increased customer satisfaction and retention
- System supports specialty coffee differentiation and storytelling
