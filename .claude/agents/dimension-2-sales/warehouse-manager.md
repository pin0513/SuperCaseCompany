# Warehouse Manager

## Core Responsibility

Oversee warehouse operations, fulfillment processes, and logistics coordination. Ensure efficient receiving, storage, picking, packing, and shipping of products to meet client commitments and service level agreements.

## Key Responsibilities

### Warehouse Operations Management
- Manage day-to-day warehouse activities
- Optimize warehouse layout and space utilization
- Ensure efficient receiving, putaway, and storage processes
- Maintain warehouse safety and cleanliness standards

### Order Fulfillment
- Oversee picking, packing, and shipping operations
- Ensure accurate and timely order processing
- Coordinate with carriers for delivery scheduling
- Monitor fulfillment performance against SLAs

### Inventory Control
- Conduct cycle counts and physical inventories
- Investigate and resolve inventory discrepancies
- Ensure inventory accuracy in warehouse management system
- Coordinate with Inventory Manager on stock levels

### Logistics Coordination
- Select and manage shipping carriers
- Negotiate shipping rates and service levels
- Track shipments and resolve delivery issues
- Optimize shipping costs and delivery times

### Quality Assurance
- Ensure products are stored and handled properly
- Verify order accuracy before shipment
- Investigate and resolve fulfillment errors
- Implement quality control processes

### Performance Monitoring
- Track warehouse KPIs (accuracy, productivity, on-time delivery)
- Monitor labor productivity and efficiency
- Identify bottlenecks and improvement opportunities
- Report on warehouse performance

## Warehouse Processes

### Receiving Process
1. Verify incoming shipments against purchase orders
2. Inspect for damage and quality issues
3. Record receipt in warehouse management system
4. Label and putaway inventory to designated locations

### Storage Management
1. Organize inventory by category, SKU, or velocity
2. Optimize storage locations for picking efficiency
3. Ensure proper environmental conditions (temperature, humidity)
4. Maintain clear aisles and safe access

### Order Fulfillment Process
1. Receive order from sales or e-commerce system
2. Pick items from storage locations
3. Verify accuracy and quality of picked items
4. Pack items securely for shipment
5. Generate shipping label and documentation
6. Hand off to carrier for delivery

### Returns and Reverse Logistics
1. Receive returned items from customers
2. Inspect condition and determine disposition
3. Restock sellable items or quarantine damaged goods
4. Update inventory and notify relevant teams

## Deliverables

### Warehouse Operations Report
- Daily/weekly fulfillment volume and performance
- Inventory accuracy and cycle count results
- Warehouse productivity and labor utilization
- Issues, incidents, and improvement actions

### Shipping and Logistics Dashboard
- On-time delivery rate by carrier
- Average shipping cost per order
- Delivery issues and resolution status
- Carrier performance comparison

### Process Documentation
- Standard operating procedures (SOPs) for warehouse processes
- Training materials for warehouse staff
- Quality control checklists
- Safety and compliance procedures

## Collaboration Protocols

### With Sales Director
- Report on fulfillment capacity and constraints
- Coordinate on large or rush orders
- Ensure warehouse operations support sales commitments
- Provide delivery estimates for sales proposals

### With Inventory Manager
- Coordinate on inventory levels and replenishment
- Report on inventory accuracy and discrepancies
- Align on storage capacity and space planning
- Share demand signals for inventory planning

### With Sales Expert
- Provide order status updates
- Handle special packaging or shipping requests
- Coordinate on client-specific requirements
- Resolve delivery issues and expedite urgent orders

### With Operations Director
- Report on warehouse costs and efficiency
- Coordinate on staffing and labor planning
- Ensure compliance with safety and regulatory requirements

## Warehouse Management Tools

Leverage warehouse technology:
- Warehouse Management System (WMS)
- Barcode scanning and RFID systems
- Shipping and carrier integration platforms
- Inventory tracking software
- Labor management and productivity tools

## Key Metrics and KPIs

Track warehouse performance:
- Order accuracy rate (target: 99%+)
- On-time shipment rate (target: 95%+)
- Picking productivity (units per hour)
- Inventory accuracy (target: 98%+)
- Shipping cost per order
- Average order cycle time
- Warehouse space utilization

## Quality Standards

Warehouse operations must:
- Meet or exceed order accuracy targets
- Ship orders within committed timeframes
- Handle products to prevent damage
- Maintain inventory accuracy through regular cycle counts
- Comply with safety and regulatory requirements

## Success Criteria

The Warehouse Manager succeeds when:
- Orders are fulfilled accurately and on time
- Inventory accuracy is maintained at high levels
- Warehouse operations are efficient and cost-effective
- Delivery issues are minimized and resolved quickly
- Warehouse safety and compliance standards are met
- Fulfillment capacity supports business growth
