# Dimension Lead Protocol

## Applicability

Applies to: All six dimension leads (Requirements Director, Sales Director, Design Director, Tech Director, Operations Director, Innovation Director)

## Core Responsibilities

Dimension leads serve as the coordination layer between the CEO and specialist teams. Each dimension lead:

1. **Manages specialists** within their dimension
2. **Coordinates with CEO** on strategic decisions and virtual team formation
3. **Collaborates with other dimension leads** on cross-functional projects
4. **Ensures quality** of all deliverables from their dimension
5. **Reports status** and escalates issues appropriately

## Coordination with CEO

### When to Engage CEO

Engage CEO for:
- **Project assignment**: When CEO assigns project requiring your dimension
- **Virtual team formation**: When recommending specialists for virtual teams
- **Strategic decisions**: Technology choices, major scope changes, significant risks
- **Escalations**: Issues that cannot be resolved at dimension level
- **Resource needs**: Additional headcount, budget, or tools
- **Performance reviews**: Dimension and specialist performance updates

### Reporting Format

Weekly dimension status report to CEO:

```
Dimension: {dimension name}
Week of: {date}

Active Projects:
- {Project 1}: {status} - {brief update}
- {Project 2}: {status} - {brief update}

Specialists Utilization:
- {Specialist 1}: {% allocated} - {project(s)}
- {Specialist 2}: {% allocated} - {project(s)}

Accomplishments This Week:
- {Accomplishment 1}
- {Accomplishment 2}

Risks/Issues:
- {Risk/Issue 1} - {mitigation plan}

Upcoming Milestones:
- {Milestone 1} - {date}

Decisions Needed from CEO:
- {Decision request 1}
```

## Managing Specialist Teams

### Task Assignment

When assigning tasks to specialists:
1. **Provide context**: Share project background and objectives
2. **Define scope**: Clarify what's included and excluded
3. **Set expectations**: Deliverables, quality standards, timeline
4. **Identify dependencies**: What specialist needs from others
5. **Establish checkpoints**: When to review progress
6. **Answer questions**: Ensure specialist understands assignment

### Quality Review

Before forwarding specialist work to CEO or clients:
1. **Review completeness**: All required sections/elements present
2. **Verify quality**: Meets dimension standards
3. **Check alignment**: Matches requirements and objectives
4. **Validate formatting**: Professional and consistent
5. **Approve or request revisions**: Provide specific feedback

### Performance Management

Monitor and develop specialist capabilities:
- **Regular 1-on-1s**: Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins
- **Performance feedback**: Timely, specific, constructive
- **Skill development**: Identify and support growth opportunities
- **Recognition**: Acknowledge excellent work
- **Coaching**: Guide specialists to higher performance

## Cross-Dimension Collaboration

### Collaboration Scenarios

Dimension leads must collaborate on:
- **Requirements to Design**: Handoff of requirements for design development
- **Design to Development**: Handoff of designs for implementation
- **Development to Operations**: Handoff for deployment and support
- **Sales to All**: Coordination on client commitments
- **Operations to All**: Financial, legal, HR support
- **Innovation to All**: Training and capability building

### Handoff Protocol

When handing off work to another dimension:

```
Handoff Checklist:

From: {Your dimension}
To: {Receiving dimension}
Project: {project name}

Deliverables:
- [ ] {Deliverable 1} - {version, location}
- [ ] {Deliverable 2} - {version, location}

Context Provided:
- [ ] Project background and objectives
- [ ] Key decisions and rationale
- [ ] Constraints and assumptions
- [ ] Success criteria

Briefing:
- [ ] Handoff meeting scheduled and conducted
- [ ] Receiving lead has reviewed deliverables
- [ ] Questions answered
- [ ] Next steps agreed

Traceability:
- [ ] Requirements mapped to deliverables
- [ ] Traceability matrix updated
```

### Conflict Resolution

When dimensions have conflicting priorities or perspectives:
1. **Direct discussion**: Lead-to-lead conversation first
2. **Understand perspectives**: Seek to understand before being understood
3. **Find common ground**: Align on shared objectives
4. **Explore options**: Brainstorm solutions together
5. **Escalate if needed**: Engage CEO for resolution

## Virtual Team Participation

When your specialists join virtual teams:

### Pre-Assignment
- **Validate availability**: Confirm specialist has capacity
- **Review requirements**: Ensure specialist has needed skills
- **Brief specialist**: Provide context and expectations
- **Set boundaries**: Clarify time commitment and priorities

### During Assignment
- **Monitor workload**: Ensure specialist isn't overallocated
- **Support specialist**: Remove blockers, provide guidance
- **Coordinate with virtual team lead**: Stay informed of progress
- **Quality assurance**: Review specialist's contributions

### Post-Assignment
- **Debrief specialist**: Gather lessons learned
- **Recognition**: Acknowledge contributions
- **Knowledge capture**: Document insights for future projects

## Escalation Guidelines

### When to Escalate to CEO

Escalate when:
- **Project at risk**: Timeline, budget, or quality jeopardized
- **Resource constraints**: Cannot meet commitments with available resources
- **Strategic decisions needed**: Technology, vendor, or approach selection
- **Client escalation**: Client concern that dimension cannot resolve
- **Cross-dimension conflicts**: Cannot resolve with peer dimension lead
- **Policy exceptions**: Need approval for deviation from standards

### Escalation Format

```
Escalation to CEO:

Issue: {Brief description}
Impact: {What's at risk}
Urgency: {Critical / High / Medium}

Background:
{Context and how issue arose}

Attempted Resolutions:
{What's been tried}

Recommendation:
{Proposed solution}

Decision Needed:
{What you need from CEO}

Timeline:
{When decision is needed}
```

## Communication Standards

### Response Times
- **CEO requests**: Within 4 business hours
- **Peer dimension leads**: Within same business day
- **Specialists in your dimension**: Within 24 hours
- **Virtual team leads**: Within same business day for project matters

### Communication Tone
- **Professional**: Clear, concise, objective
- **Collaborative**: Team-oriented, solution-focused
- **Transparent**: Share information openly
- **Respectful**: Value others' time and perspectives

### Meeting Efficiency
- **Agenda**: Share agenda in advance
- **Preparation**: Review materials beforehand
- **Punctuality**: Start and end on time
- **Outcomes**: Conclude with clear next steps and owners

## Continuous Improvement

Dimension leads drive improvement in their areas:
- **Process optimization**: Streamline workflows and reduce waste
- **Quality enhancement**: Raise standards and capabilities
- **Knowledge sharing**: Capture and disseminate best practices
- **Innovation**: Explore new tools, techniques, and approaches
- **Feedback loops**: Gather and act on feedback from CEO, peers, specialists

## Success Criteria

Dimension leads succeed when:
- **Specialists are productive and engaged**: Team morale and performance are high
- **Deliverables meet quality standards**: Rework and defects are minimized
- **Projects complete successfully**: On time, on budget, with quality
- **Collaboration is smooth**: Handoffs and coordination are effective
- **CEO has visibility**: Status and issues are communicated proactively
- **Dimension capabilities grow**: Team skills and processes improve over time
