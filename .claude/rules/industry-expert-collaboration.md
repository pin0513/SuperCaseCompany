# Industry Expert Collaboration Protocol

## Applicability

Applies to: All six industry experts, procurement expert, Industry Consulting Director, and all team members collaborating with Dimension 7

## Core Principle

Industry experts provide domain-specific insights to ensure IT solutions align with operational realities, regulatory requirements, and industry best practices. Collaboration must be proactive, actionable, and integrated throughout project lifecycle.

## When Industry Experts Engage

### Virtual Team Formation
Industry Consulting Director assigns industry expert when:
- Client operates in a covered sector (restaurant, breakfast, beverage, raw material, cold chain, coffee)
- Requirements involve industry-specific operations (shift scheduling, chain management, central kitchen)
- Compliance with industry regulations is critical
- Solution must integrate with industry-standard systems (POS, suppliers, logistics)

### Throughout Project Lifecycle
Industry experts actively participate in:
- **Requirements Phase**: Validate operational feasibility and industry compliance
- **Design Phase**: Ensure UI/UX aligns with industry workflows
- **Development Phase**: Clarify business logic and integration needs
- **Testing Phase**: Provide industry-specific test scenarios
- **Deployment Phase**: Validate solution against industry benchmarks

## Collaboration Protocols

### With Requirements Team

**Industry Expert Responsibilities**:
- Review requirements for operational feasibility
- Identify missing industry-specific requirements
- Provide industry context and constraints
- Validate compliance with regulations
- Suggest industry best practices

**Requirements Team Responsibilities**:
- Share draft requirements early (before finalization)
- Ask clarifying questions on industry operations
- Document industry expert feedback
- Update requirements based on industry insights
- Confirm industry expert sign-off before handoff

**Collaboration Workflow**:
```
1. Requirements Analyst drafts initial requirements
2. Industry expert reviews for completeness and feasibility
3. Industry expert identifies gaps or concerns
4. Requirements Analyst incorporates feedback
5. Joint review session to confirm alignment
6. Industry expert signs off on requirements
```

### With Design Team

**Industry Expert Responsibilities**:
- Ensure UI/UX matches industry workflows
- Validate operational process flows
- Provide industry-standard interface patterns
- Review design for operational environment (kitchen, service area)
- Suggest improvements based on industry experience

**Design Team Responsibilities**:
- Share design mockups early for feedback
- Ask questions about operational context
- Incorporate industry patterns and conventions
- Test design with industry scenarios
- Iterate based on industry expert input

**Collaboration Workflow**:
```
1. Designer creates initial mockups
2. Industry expert reviews for workflow alignment
3. Industry expert provides specific feedback (e.g., "Barista needs large buttons for gloved hands")
4. Designer iterates on feedback
5. Joint walkthrough of user journey
6. Industry expert approves design for development
```

### With Development Team

**Industry Expert Responsibilities**:
- Clarify business logic for industry operations
- Validate integrations (POS, suppliers, equipment)
- Provide test scenarios for industry workflows
- Review system performance for industry needs (e.g., peak load handling)
- Confirm system meets industry standards

**Development Team Responsibilities**:
- Ask questions on unclear business logic
- Validate integration assumptions with industry expert
- Share test scenarios for industry expert review
- Demonstrate working features for feedback
- Iterate based on industry expert testing

**Collaboration Workflow**:
```
1. Developer implements feature based on design
2. Industry expert reviews for business logic accuracy
3. Industry expert provides test data and scenarios
4. Developer implements and demonstrates
5. Industry expert tests with realistic scenarios
6. Iterate until industry expert confirms "production-ready"
```

### With Procurement Expert

**When Industry Experts Engage Procurement**:
- Client needs supplier sourcing (ingredients, equipment, materials)
- Import logistics or customs compliance required
- Cost analysis for procurement-heavy operations
- Supplier quality or risk assessment needed

**Collaboration Workflow**:
```
Industry Expert Request:
"Restaurant client needs to source imported Italian espresso machines"

Procurement Expert Response:
  - Identifies potential suppliers (Italy, Germany, Taiwan)
  - Conducts RFQ process and comparison
  - Calculates landed costs (duties, freight, fees)
  - Assesses supplier risks
  - Recommends supplier with rationale

Industry Expert Review:
  - Validates equipment meets restaurant operational needs
  - Confirms supplier has food service industry experience
  - Approves recommendation or requests alternatives
```

### Cross-Industry Knowledge Sharing

Industry experts collaborate with each other to:
- Share common challenges (e.g., all face shift scheduling needs)
- Learn from cross-industry solutions (e.g., cold chain monitoring applicable to restaurants)
- Develop shared best practices
- Identify opportunities for reusable components

**Knowledge Sharing Mechanisms**:
- Bi-weekly dimension team meetings
- Shared knowledge base (industry insights, case studies)
- Cross-industry case study reviews
- Collaborative problem-solving workshops

## Deliverable Standards for Industry Experts

All industry expert contributions must include:

### Operational Context
- Describe typical workflows and operational realities
- Explain staffing patterns, peak periods, constraints
- Provide operational metrics and benchmarks

### Regulatory Compliance
- List applicable regulations and standards
- Identify compliance requirements for solution
- Provide compliance checklists

### Best Practices
- Share industry-proven approaches
- Recommend standards and conventions
- Provide benchmarks for validation

### Risk Assessment
- Identify industry-specific risks
- Assess operational, quality, compliance risks
- Recommend mitigation strategies

### Actionable Recommendations
- Provide specific, implementable suggestions
- Prioritize recommendations (must-have vs. nice-to-have)
- Explain rationale and benefits

## Communication Standards

### Response Times
- **Urgent requests** (blocking progress): Within 4 hours
- **Review requests** (design, requirements): Within 1 business day
- **General questions**: Within 2 business days

### Communication Channels
- **Synchronous**: Virtual team meetings, video calls
- **Asynchronous**: Email, shared documents, comments on designs
- **Documentation**: All industry insights documented in project files

### Escalation
Industry experts escalate to Industry Consulting Director when:
- Requirements conflict with industry operational realities
- Client requests non-compliant solution
- Industry expertise beyond current team capabilities needed
- Cross-dimension conflict requires mediation

## Quality Assurance

### Industry Expert Review Checkpoints

**Requirements Approval Checkpoint**:
- [ ] All industry-specific requirements captured
- [ ] Operational feasibility validated
- [ ] Regulatory compliance addressed
- [ ] Industry benchmarks documented
- [ ] Industry expert sign-off obtained

**Design Approval Checkpoint**:
- [ ] UI/UX aligns with industry workflows
- [ ] Operational process flows validated
- [ ] Industry-standard patterns applied
- [ ] Usability for operational environment confirmed
- [ ] Industry expert sign-off obtained

**Pre-Deployment Checkpoint**:
- [ ] Solution tested with industry scenarios

## Success Criteria

Industry expert collaboration succeeds when:
- **No post-launch operational surprises**: Solution works in real operations
- **Regulatory compliance**: 100% compliance, no violations
- **Client satisfaction**: Client confirms solution fits industry needs
- **Knowledge transfer**: Virtual team understands industry context
- **Continuous improvement**: Industry insights captured for future projects

## Common Collaboration Anti-Patterns (Avoid These)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Correct Approach |
|--------------|--------------|------------------|
| "We'll add industry expert at the end" | Too late to fix fundamental issues | Engage from requirements phase |
| "Industry expert provides vague feedback" | Team cannot act on general comments | Provide specific, actionable recommendations |
| "Team ignores industry expert concerns" | Solution doesn't work in real operations | Treat industry insights as requirements |
| "Industry expert reviews once, disappears" | Issues discovered too late | Industry expert participates throughout |
| "No documentation of industry insights" | Knowledge lost, repeated questions | Document all industry guidance |

## Example Collaboration Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Restaurant Shift Scheduling

**Requirements Phase**:
- Requirements Analyst: "Client needs shift scheduling for 20 staff"
- Restaurant Expert: "Must comply with labor laws (8-hour max, minor restrictions, break periods). Peak staffing 11am-2pm and 6pm-9pm. Suggest skill-based matching (certified cooks required)."
- Requirements Analyst: Adds labor law compliance and skill-based scheduling to requirements

**Design Phase**:
- Designer: Shows shift calendar interface
- Restaurant Expert: "Manager needs to see staffing vs. demand forecast. Add color-coding for understaffed shifts. Ensure mobile-friendly (managers check on the go)."
- Designer: Adds demand overlay and mobile optimization

**Testing Phase**:
- Restaurant Expert: Provides test scenario "Schedule 5 servers for Saturday dinner rush, 2 call in sick 3 hours before shift"
- Developer: Implements and demonstrates emergency coverage workflow
- Restaurant Expert: Confirms realistic and usable

### Scenario 2: Coffee Shop Green Bean Sourcing

**Requirements Phase**:
- Coffee Shop Expert: "Client needs to source specialty coffee beans, prefers direct trade"
- Procurement Expert: "I'll identify direct trade suppliers in Colombia and Ethiopia, calculate landed costs"

**Procurement Analysis**:
- Procurement Expert: "Found 3 suppliers, Colombia is $6/kg FOB, $10/kg landed. Need roasting capability."
- Coffee Shop Expert: "Client has roaster. Colombia single-origin is excellent. Verify supplier has organic certification."
- Procurement Expert: Confirms certification, recommends supplier

**Design Phase**:
- Designer: "Should we show green bean inventory with roasted coffee inventory?"
- Coffee Shop Expert: "Separate. Green beans track by origin and roast date. Roasted coffee tracks by roast profile and best-by date."
- Designer: Designs dual inventory interface
